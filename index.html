The Wind Waker & Why We Play
In my analysis, I want to analyse the narrative devices and other techniques used in "The Legend of Zelda: The Wind Waker" that drive the emotions of the story and compare them with Massumi's take on "aspect vs. emotion".

To do this, I want to examine a cutscene that takes place early in the game, depicting the key event that sets the story in motion.

This clip shows the protagonist of the game, Link, seeing his younger sister, Aryll, from across a bridge. As Aryll crosses the bridge to meet with her brother, however, a giant bird creature swoops down and steals her away, flying off into the distance.

Massumi categorizes affect and emotion as two distinct, but connected phenomena--affect as an instinctual, visceral reaction, and emotion as a more categorized, processed response.
